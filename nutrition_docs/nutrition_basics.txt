MACRONUTRIENTS - COMPLETE GUIDE

PROTEINS:
- Adults need minimum 0.8g protein per kg body weight daily
- Athletes and muscle gainers need 1.6-2.2g per kg body weight
- High protein foods: chicken breast (31g/100g), eggs (13g each),
  lentils (9g/100g), Greek yogurt (10g/100g), tuna (30g/100g),
  paneer (18g/100g), chickpeas (9g/100g), tofu (8g/100g)
- Protein helps build muscle, repair tissue, boost metabolism
- Protein takes longer to digest keeping you full longer

CARBOHYDRATES:
- Primary energy source for brain and muscles
- Complex carbs (slow release): brown rice, oats, sweet potato,
  quinoa, whole wheat bread, barley, lentils
- Simple carbs (fast release): white bread, sugar, candy, juice
- For weight loss: choose complex carbs, avoid simple carbs
- For muscle gain: eat carbs around workouts
- Daily target: 45-65% of total calories

FATS:
- Essential for hormones, vitamins A/D/E/K absorption, brain health
- Healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, nuts, salmon, seeds
- Unhealthy fats: fried food, processed snacks, margarine
- Daily target: 20-35% of total calories
- Never go below 20% fat - hormones depend on it

FIBER:
- Adults need 25-38g of fiber daily
- High fiber foods: beans, lentils, oats, fruits, vegetables
- Fiber feeds good gut bacteria, reduces cholesterol, controls blood sugar
- Drink water when eating high fiber foods

WATER AND HYDRATION:
- Adults need 8-10 glasses (2-2.5 liters) of water daily
- More needed during exercise, hot weather, illness
- Signs of dehydration: dark urine, headache, fatigue, dizziness
- Coffee and tea count but also cause some fluid loss
- Eat water-rich foods: cucumber, watermelon, oranges
