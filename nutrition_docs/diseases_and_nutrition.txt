NUTRITION FOR HEALTH CONDITIONS

OBESITY:
- Obesity is BMI above 30
- Caused by: calorie surplus, sedentary lifestyle, genetics,
  hormonal issues, medications, poor sleep
- Treatment: gradual calorie deficit, increased activity,
  behavioral changes, medical support if needed
- Best foods: vegetables, lean protein, whole grains, legumes
- Avoid: processed food, sugary drinks, fried food, alcohol
- Even 5-10% body weight loss significantly reduces health risks

TYPE 2 DIABETES AND NUTRITION:
- Control blood sugar by choosing low glycemic index foods
- Low GI foods: oats, legumes, most vegetables, berries, quinoa
- High GI foods to limit: white bread, white rice, sugary drinks
- Eat regular meals to prevent blood sugar spikes and drops
- Include fiber at every meal to slow sugar absorption
- Limit: refined carbs, sugary foods, fruit juices, alcohol
- Best foods: leafy greens, eggs, chicken, fish, nuts, berries

HEART DISEASE AND NUTRITION:
- Reduce saturated fat: limit red meat, full-fat dairy, fried food
- Eat omega-3 fatty acids: salmon, sardines, walnuts, flaxseeds
- Increase soluble fiber: oats, apples, lentils reduce cholesterol
- Reduce sodium to lower blood pressure: limit salt, processed food
- Eat more: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, fish
- Avoid: trans fats, processed meats, high sodium foods, alcohol

HYPERTENSION (HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE):
- Follow DASH diet: Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension
- Reduce sodium intake to under 2300mg per day
- Increase potassium: bananas, sweet potato, spinach, avocado
- Eat more: fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy
- Limit: salt, processed food, alcohol, caffeine, red meat

ANEMIA (LOW IRON):
- Iron deficiency is most common nutritional deficiency globally
- Best iron sources: red meat, lentils, spinach, fortified cereals
- Eat vitamin C with iron sources to increase absorption
- Avoid tea/coffee with iron-rich meals (reduces absorption)
- Vegetarians at higher risk - need to focus on plant iron sources

PCOD/PCOS AND NUTRITION:
- Common hormonal disorder affecting women
- Focus on low glycemic index foods to control insulin
- Increase fiber intake to balance hormones
- Reduce processed carbs, sugar, dairy (for some women)
- Anti-inflammatory foods help: berries, leafy greens, olive oil
- Maintain healthy weight as even small loss improves symptoms

THYROID AND NUTRITION:
- Hypothyroidism: avoid raw cruciferous vegetables in excess
- Get adequate iodine: seaweed, fish, dairy, iodized salt
- Selenium helps thyroid: Brazil nuts, eggs, sunflower seeds
- Take thyroid medication on empty stomach, wait 30-60 min before eating

DIGESTIVE ISSUES AND IBS:
- Follow low FODMAP diet for IBS
- Avoid: onions, garlic, wheat, apples, dairy (high FODMAP)
- Safe foods: rice, oats, carrots, spinach, chicken, fish, eggs
- Eat smaller meals more frequently
- Identify personal trigger foods through elimination diet
- Probiotics may help: yogurt, kefir, kimchi, sauerkraut
