PAKISTANI AND SOUTH ASIAN NUTRITION GUIDE

COMMON PAKISTANI FOODS AND NUTRITION:

DAAL (LENTILS):
- One of the best protein sources, especially for vegetarians
- Masoor dal: 9g protein per 100g cooked, high iron and folate
- Chana dal: 8g protein, high fiber, low glycemic index
- Moong dal: 7g protein, easily digestible, good for all ages
- Eat with roti for complete protein (combining plant proteins)

ROTI AND BREAD:
- Whole wheat roti is much better than white flour (maida)
- One whole wheat roti: 70-80 calories, 3g protein, 2g fiber
- Maida roti spikes blood sugar - limit for diabetes and weight loss
- Better alternatives: barley roti (jowar), millet roti (bajra)

RICE:
- White rice is fine in moderation but brown rice is better
- Cook and cool rice then reheat - creates resistant starch
  which feeds good gut bacteria
- Portion control: one cup cooked rice per meal is reasonable
- Pair with protein and vegetables to balance the meal

BIRYANI AND PILAF:
- High calorie - mostly rice, oil, and sometimes fatty meat
- Healthier version: use brown rice, less oil, lean meat, more vegetables
- Occasional food for weight management - not daily

CHICKEN CURRY:
- Good protein source but watch the oil content
- Skin contains more fat - remove for weight loss
- Healthier cooking: bake or grill instead of fry

SABZI (VEGETABLES):
- Pakistani cuisine has excellent vegetable dishes
- Saag (spinach) is extremely nutritious: iron, calcium, vitamins
- Karela (bitter gourd) excellent for blood sugar control
- Tinda, tori, kaddu all low calorie and nutritious
- Problem: often cooked in too much oil - reduce for health

LASSI AND YOGURT (DAHI):
- Plain yogurt is excellent probiotic, protein source
- Full-fat dahi occasionally fine, low-fat for weight management
- Sweet lassi very high in sugar - limit
- Plain lassi is a good recovery drink after exercise

CHAI (TEA):
- Heavy milk chai with sugar has 100-150 calories per cup
- Drink fewer cups if trying to lose weight
- Green tea is much healthier alternative - antioxidants, boosts metabolism
- Avoid drinking tea immediately after iron-rich meals

COMMON ISSUES IN PAKISTANI DIET:
- Too much oil in cooking (reduce to 1-2 tablespoons per dish)
- Too much sugar in tea and desserts
- Not enough vegetables at meals
- Large portions of rice and roti
- Deep fried snacks (samosa, pakora) as regular snacks
- Lack of regular meal times

HEALTHY PAKISTANI MEAL TEMPLATE:
Breakfast: eggs + whole wheat roti + chai (less sugar)
Lunch: daal + sabzi + 1 cup rice or 2 roti + salad
Dinner: chicken or fish + sabzi + 1-2 roti
Snacks: fruit, nuts, plain dahi

RAMADAN NUTRITION TIPS:
- Break fast with dates and water (traditional and nutritious)
- Avoid fried foods at iftar - very hard on empty stomach
- Eat sehri with complex carbs and protein for sustained energy
- Stay hydrated between iftar and sehri
- Best sehri: eggs, oats, whole wheat roti, yogurt, water
